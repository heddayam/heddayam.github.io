\section*{Experience}
\vspace{4pt}
\noindent{\large\textbf{Doctoral Researcher, University of Chicago CS}} \hfill 2021--2026
\begin{cvitems}
    \item {[ongoing]} Developing \textbf{AI to support critical thinking and civic deliberation} through \href{https://civicchats.org}{CivicChats.org}. Built an automated evaluation pipeline to detect hallucinations, bias, and stereotyping through \textbf{iterative prompt optimization over collected multi-turn user data}. Follow-up work on \textbf{large-scale data curation and synthetic data generation for post-training} to elicit coherent perspectives in models to support more productive user interactions. Wrote about this effort \href{https://open.substack.com/pub/cichicago/p/civicchats-building-ai-to-support}{here}.
    \item Developed a method for \textbf{at-scale automatic extraction of causal narratives and applied it to 4.2M sentences} from a century of U.S.\ inflation coverage [2]. Found that causal narratives predict consumer expectations and spread via contagion and differentiationf[2]---\textbf{demonstrating how language shapes economic outcomes at societal scale.} Presented at \href{https://www.hoover.org}{Stanford Hoover Institute}.
    \item Showed that language reduces price variance by 3x, through experimental design and controlled experiment ($\sim$400 participants). Found that successful buyers use empathetic framing while pushing prices down whereas sellers succeed by asking user needs early in the conversation [3].
    \item Built a 25.6K \textbf{long-context understanding benchmark} from Supreme Court opinions [4]. Showed fine-tuning improves automatic metrics while increasing hallucination rate. Found that a) \textbf{frontier models hallucinate less but more subtly, creating critical safety challenges in mitigating their risk,} and b) neither standard metrics nor LLM-as-judge reliably correlate with human judgement.
\end{cvitems}

\vspace{4pt}
\noindent{\large\textbf{ML Research Scientist Intern, \href{https://www.abridge.com}{Abridge AI}}} \hfill Summer 2025
\begin{cvitems}
    \item Demonstrated that chain-of-thought internalization fails on complex reasoning [1]. Proposed \textbf{post-think reasoning distillation}, a simple yet effective compression target that preserves the teacher's solution path and achieves the \textbf{best accuracy--efficiency trade-off}. Motived by the need for faster inference that tightens the human oversight loop in \textbf{safety critical healthcare settings}.
\end{cvitems}
